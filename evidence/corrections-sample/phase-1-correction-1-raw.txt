### 5.9 AI Risk: Platform Does Not Depend on AI-Driven Decision Making

**Description:** Ocean Golf has explicitly determined that AI is NOT a core component of the platform (manifest shows ai_dependency: 'None'). This decision means that personalized recommendations, course selection, and client matching are driven by Rafael's human expertise and judgment, not by machine learning models or algorithmic curation. This is a strategic choice that aligns with the premium, relationship-driven positioning, but it carries an implicit risk: if Rafael's expertise becomes a bottleneck and clients begin to expect algorithmic personalization (as competitors may offer), the platform could appear less sophisticated compared to AI-augmented alternatives.

**Severity:** Low (non-critical in the near term, but worth monitoring as luxury travel markets evolve). The risk is not that AI is missing, but that client expectations for intelligent automation may increase faster than Ocean Golf's capability to deliver without AI.

**Current Mitigation Thinking:** (1) Curation-first positioning explicitly emphasizes that Rafael's expertise is the differentiator, not algorithmic matching. (2) Data collection is structured to enable Rafael's decision-making, not to train models independently. (3) If client expectations shift significantly toward AI-driven recommendations (validated through customer research in Phase 9), Ocean Golf has optionality to add AI features in Phase 2+ without disrupting the core business. (4) The platform architecture does not prohibit AI; it simply does not assume it. This keeps the door open for future AI integration without early-stage complexity.</search_text>
